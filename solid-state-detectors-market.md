<p><p>The solid-state detectors market comprises two primary types: semiconductor detectors and diamond detectors. Semiconductor detectors, commonly made from materials like silicon or germanium, are widely used for radiation sensing due to their sensitivity and compact size. Diamond detectors, known for their high radiation resistance and excellent thermal conductivity, are increasingly utilized in applications requiring robustness and accuracy, such as in high-energy physics and medical imaging. Both types enhance detection capabilities across various industries, including healthcare, nuclear, and security sectors.</p></p>
