<p><p>The Automotive Split-view Camera Module market utilizes two primary sensor technologies: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) chips. CCD chips are known for their high image quality, low noise, and superior performance in low-light conditions, making them suitable for premium automotive applications. CMOS chips, on the other hand, are cost-effective, energy-efficient, and offer faster processing speeds, making them ideal for mass-market vehicles. Both technologies enhance driver safety by providing clear, real-time split-view imaging for parking, maneuvering, and obstacle detection.</p></p>
