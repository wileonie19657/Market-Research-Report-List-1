<p><p>The chilli oil market features two primary types: hot pressed and cold pressed. Hot pressed chilli oil is produced by extracting oils from chillies using heat, resulting in a robust flavor and vibrant color, often preferred for cooking. In contrast, cold pressed chilli oil is made without heat, preserving the natural essence of the chillies, resulting in a milder taste with complex aromas. Both types cater to different culinary needs, appealing to diverse consumer preferences in flavor intensity and cooking applications.</p></p>
