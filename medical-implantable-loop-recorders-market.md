<p><p>Medical implantable loop recorders (ILRs) serve to monitor heart rhythms over extended periods. The market is divided into two primary types: manual and automatic. Manual ILRs require healthcare professionals to trigger recording events, making them suitable for patients experiencing sporadic symptoms. In contrast, automatic ILRs continuously monitor heart activity and autonomously record abnormal rhythms, enhancing efficiency in diagnosing conditions like arrhythmias. Both types play crucial roles in improving patient outcomes through timely and accurate cardiac monitoring.</p></p>
