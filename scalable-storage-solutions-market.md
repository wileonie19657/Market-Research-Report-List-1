<p><p>Scalable storage solutions can be categorized into horizontal and vertical expansion. Horizontal expansion involves adding more storage nodes or systems to increase capacity, allowing for seamless scaling across multiple devices and locations. This approach enhances redundancy and performance through distributed architectures. Conversely, vertical expansion focuses on upgrading existing storage systems by adding more resources, such as increased disk space or improved hardware, to enhance performance and capacity within a single unit. Both methods cater to growing data needs efficiently.</p></p>
