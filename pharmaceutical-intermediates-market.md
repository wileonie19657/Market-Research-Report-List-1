<p><p>The pharmaceutical intermediates market is categorized into GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice) and non-GMP segments. GMP intermediates are produced under strict regulatory guidelines to ensure quality and safety, making them essential for the manufacture of pharmaceutical products intended for human use. In contrast, non-GMP intermediates are manufactured without stringent regulations and are typically used for research and development, or in veterinary applications. The distinction between the two reflects the varying quality standards and regulatory oversight in pharmaceutical production.</p></p>
