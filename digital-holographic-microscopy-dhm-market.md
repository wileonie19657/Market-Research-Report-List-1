<p><p>Digital Holographic Microscopy (DHM) is an advanced imaging technique that captures the interference patterns of light scattered from samples, enabling the visualization of cellular structures without the need for labels. The market includes two primary types: the Label-Free Interferometric Technique, which simplifies sample preparation by allowing real-time imaging without dyes or labels, and the Quantitative Phase Image market, which focuses on extracting quantitative data from phase shifts to analyze cell morphology and dynamics, facilitating applications in biology and materials science.</p></p>
