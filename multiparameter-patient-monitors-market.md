<p><ul><li>High-acuity Monitors</li><li>Mid-acuity Monitors</li><li>Low-acuity Monitors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Multiparameter patient monitors are categorized into three main types based on the acuity of patient care. High-acuity monitors are designed for critically ill patients, providing extensive parameters like cardiac output and advanced vital signs. Mid-acuity monitors are suited for patients requiring less intensive monitoring, such as those in general medical wards, focusing on essential vitals. Low-acuity monitors are used in outpatient settings or during routine check-ups, typically tracking basic parameters like heart rate and blood pressure. Each type serves specific clinical needs.</p></p>
