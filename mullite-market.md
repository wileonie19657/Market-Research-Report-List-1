<p><ul><li>Natural Mullite</li><li>Fused Mullite</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The mullite market consists of two primary types: natural mullite and fused mullite. Natural mullite is sourced from naturally occurring deposits and is valued for its high thermal stability and low thermal expansion, making it ideal for applications in ceramics, refractories, and metallurgy. Fused mullite, on the other hand, is produced by melting alumina and silica in an electric furnace, resulting in a purer and more uniform product, suitable for advanced refractory materials and high-performance ceramics. Both types cater to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
