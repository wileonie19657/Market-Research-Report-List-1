<p><p>The Glucose Dehydrogenase market comprises two primary types: FAD-Glucose Dehydrogenase and PQQ-Glucose Dehydrogenase. FAD-Glucose Dehydrogenase utilizes flavin adenine dinucleotide as a cofactor, playing a crucial role in glucose oxidation and is often employed in biosensors and diagnostics. Conversely, PQQ-Glucose Dehydrogenase uses pyrroloquinoline quinone, offering advantages in catalytic efficiency and stability, making it beneficial for applications in biotechnological processes. Both types are vital in various industries, including healthcare and food processing, for glucose detection and metabolism.</p></p>
