<p><ul><li>Protopectinase</li><li>Polygalacturonase</li><li>Pectin Lyase</li><li>Pectinesterase</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The food grade pectinase market comprises various enzyme types, each serving distinct functions. Protopectinase breaks down protopectin into pectin, aiding in juice extraction. Polygalacturonase degrades pectin chains, enhancing clarity and yield in fruit juices and wines. Pectin lyase contributes to texture modification in fruit products by depolymerizing pectin, while pectinesterase reduces bitterness and improves flavor by controlling pectin methylation. Together, these enzymes play critical roles in food processing and product enhancement across various industries.</p></p>
