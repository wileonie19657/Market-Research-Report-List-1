<p><p>The fat replacer market is categorized into three main types: carbohydrate-based, protein-based, and lipid-based fat replacers. Carbohydrate-based fat replacers utilize polysaccharides or fiber to mimic the texture and mouthfeel of fats, often found in low-calorie products. Protein-based fat replacers use proteins to provide creaminess and improve texture, commonly seen in dairy alternatives. Lipid-based fat replacers incorporate modified fats that retain flavor and mouthfeel while reducing calorie content, used primarily in baked goods and snacks. Each type caters to health-conscious consumers seeking reduced fat options.</p></p>
